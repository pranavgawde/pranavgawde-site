Studio

Design, engineering, and startup consulting from someone still building.

I help early-stage founders building real-world products — hardware, robotics, deep-tech, consumer goods — turn ideas into things that actually ship. I also work with the institutions, incubators, and maker communities that grow the next generation of builders.

What I consult on

Industrial & Product Design

Sketch to manufacturable CAD; form, function, and the details that decide whether a product feels cheap or considered.

Prototyping & Manufacturing

What it takes to make 1, then 10, then 100, then 1,000 — and the very different decisions each step demands.

User Research & Pilot Testing

Understanding who your user actually is, taking the prototype out to them, and iterating until it genuinely works.

Pitch & Investor Readiness

Narrative, deck, and demo strategy for founders pitching hardware to investors who default to SaaS.

Programme & Lab Design

For incubators, accelerators, and FabLabs setting up or scaling their innovation infrastructure.

Teaching & Programme Delivery

Fab Academy instruction (in partnership with Fab Foundation / MIT), guest lecturing, design juries and programme advisory.

How I work

I work in 4–8 week sprints with founders, and on retainer with institutions.

Who I work with

Founders

  • Early-stage hardware and deep-tech startups
  • Founders building physical products who need design and engineering hands on the problem
  • Teams navigating the gap between prototype and first production run
  • Entrepreneurs pitching to investors who've never touched a physical product

Institutions

  • Innovation labs, FabLabs, and maker spaces building programmes
  • Incubators and accelerators scaling their design and hardware tracks
  • Universities and design schools (juries, lectures, workshops)
  • Organisations establishing prototyping and design infrastructure from scratch
